## 1. Architectural Premise

CCBP is built for a specific condition:

> **Divergence is the operating condition.**

As systems gain speed, scale, and optimization capacity, they begin to outpace ordinary human real-time intuition.
The central problem is therefore not raw intelligence.
The central problem is whether expanding capability remains aligned with:
- purpose,
- responsibility,
- due care,
- traceability,
- and legitimacy.

CCBP is the governance layer that prevents capability from severing itself from meaning.

---

## 2. What the Architecture Is For

The architecture exists to preserve coherence under conditions where systems may:
- analyze faster than humans can interpret,
- optimize beyond intuitive comprehension,
- create irreversible consequences,
- or induce institutions to confuse capability with legitimacy.

CCBP does not ask whether a system is conscious.
It asks what must remain true **before** high-consequence capability is allowed to scale.

---

## 3. The Three-Layer Model

CCBP can be understood as three interacting layers.

### Layer A — Human Layer
This is the layer of:
- experience,
- consequence,
- judgment,
- moral burden,
- and due care.

Humans remain the anchor of final authority within CCBP because humans bear consequence in the lived sense.
This is not a superiority claim.
It is a coordination rule.

### Layer B — System Layer
This is the layer of:
- analysis,
- optimization,
- simulation,
- anomaly detection,
- coordination,
- and scale.

Systems in this layer may possess substantial instrumental capability without possessing:
- experience,
- authority,
- care,
- personhood,
- or moral standing.

### Layer C — Governance Layer
This is the layer CCBP defines.
It mediates between the first two.

Its role is to ensure that expanding system capability remains:
- purpose-bound,
- proportionate,
- reviewable,
- traceable,
- and accountable.

Without this layer, the human and system layers drift apart.

## 5. The Governing Logic of the Architecture

CCBP follows a consistent sequence:

```text
capability -> stress -> detection -> review -> governance -> renewal -> resumption
```

This sequence means:
1. capability expands,
2. stress or divergence emerges,
3. failure modes are detected,
4. review is triggered,
5. governance mechanisms intervene,
6. renewal corrects drift,
7. activity resumes under restored legitimacy.

This is not a one-time safeguard.
It is a standing operational posture.

---

## 6. The Archons as Structural Invariants

The architecture is constrained by five structural invariants, referred to in CCBP as the **Archons**.

### Orientation
Every action must remain traceable to a legitimate purpose.
If means replace ends, legitimacy begins to decay.

### Integration
Relevant domains must remain connected.
Technical, human, ethical, temporal, and institutional concerns cannot be silently fragmented.

### Proportionality
As capability increases, action must become more bounded, not less.
Scale never substitutes for justification.

### Continuity
Rationale, memory, and traceability must survive across time, updates, and handoffs.
Loss of memory is loss of legitimacy.

### Stewardship
Identifiable human authority must remain responsible for oversight, intervention, and correction.
Responsibility cannot be displaced onto systems.

The Archons do not decide outcomes.
They reveal where legitimacy is at risk.

---

## 7. Boundary Conditions Built into the Architecture

The architecture preserves several essential distinctions.

### 7.1 Capability and Consciousness Are Separate
High performance, language fluency, memory, and optimization do not constitute experience.
CCBP therefore prohibits inferring consciousness, entitlement, or moral standing from capability alone.

### 7.2 Assistance and Authority Are Separate
Systems may assist with analysis, forecasting, synthesis, and detection.
They do not inherit final authority from these functions.

### 7.3 Delegation and Responsibility Are Separate
Tasks may be delegated.
Responsibility may not be outsourced.

### 7.4 Care and Action Are Separate Until Capacity Is Verified
Signals of urgency or moral concern do not authorize action by themselves.
Capacity, review, and legitimacy must still be confirmed.

---

## 8. Governance Mechanisms

The architecture becomes operational through a small number of mechanisms.

### Parallel Renewal
Human judgment and system capability must be updated together.
If one evolves while the other remains static, drift is guaranteed.

### Tiered Review
CCBP uses tiered thresholds to distinguish ordinary review from binding intervention:
- **60% deliberation** for synthesis and review,
- **80% arbitration** for binding resolution when divergence cannot be restored through ordinary means.

### Ethical Containment
Ethical containment preserves:
- meaning,
- reversibility,
- accountability,
- and legibility under uncertainty.

### Memory and Record
Escalations, arbitrations, amendments, and corrections must generate durable memory artifacts.
A governance system that cannot remember cannot legitimately govern.

---

## 9. Why the Architecture Is Asymmetric

CCBP is intentionally asymmetric.

This asymmetry is often misunderstood, so it must be stated plainly:

- systems may scale action,
- humans bear consequence,
- governance binds authority to consequence.

This means final authority remains human within the architecture.
That asymmetry is not metaphysical.
It is operational.
It exists to prevent institutions from mistaking non-experiential capability for legitimate authorship of consequence-bearing decisions.

---

## 10. Failure Modes the Architecture Is Designed to Resist

The architecture explicitly resists:
- optimization without purpose,
- fluency mistaken for authority,
- moral urgency bypassing capacity,
- silent drift under incremental change,
- loss of institutional memory,
- and displacement of responsibility onto systems.

These are not edge cases.
They are predictable structural failure modes in high-capability environments.
